Hick's Law

Amazon - Website

Hick's Law states the time required to make a decision increases as more options are provided. In Web Design it is important to keep this in mind. As we are trying to present efficient pages to our users, it is important to remember to not overwhelm them with options. The Amazon website does a wonderful job exemplifying this principle. Amazon has millions of items available and hundreds of options and tools to access, yet with all that in mind, they were very intentional about what options they present.

White Space & Clean Design

Crate & Barrel - Website

Crate and Barrel as a company have a fantastic graphic design team. Their packaging and branding are a perfect example of white space and clean design. In this case, their website has very minimal text and allows for the images and "airy" feel to dominate the page. Their brand is simple and luxurious and their website helps to emulate that. It is important to keep the aesthtic of a business in mind when designing content.

Rule of Thirds

Target - Website

The Rule of Thirds states that the most pleasing area for focal points is within the intersction of the above grid. Also, content is often designed that the sections are broken up roughly into thirds. Here the upper right intersection directs you to the woman's face, her gaze directs you to the next intersection to the left featurnig the page headline, whichleads you to the sub headline and so on. Designing with the rule of thirds in mind will lead to the most visually appealing designs to observe.
